# Neil's Cottage

The crime scene. Neil Hartigan's home. Specifically: the kitchen, where the erasure occurs.

**The kitchen (key features for the drafter).** Neil has habitual order: each cup returns to a specific spot; the tea towel has a usual fold; the chair sits at a particular angle. He is a man of established domestic routines — a detail Maggie the potter (a maker with her own physical routines) would register.

**The erasure (planted ch 7).** The killer washed the second cup and returned it a fraction out of Neil's habitual order. Squared the chair too neatly — Neil's own sits askew. The tea towel is damp and refolded. The biscuit tin lid is fractionally misaligned. The room has been smoothed by someone hiding their presence. A normal observer sees a tidy room. Maggie's Migraine Sight sees the seams.

**What the erasure tells us (for the drafter — without naming the culprit).** The visitor knew this kitchen as well as their own — returned the cup with the unthinking familiarity of someone who has sat here many times over many years. Not a stranger; someone from history and family. Do not name who in drafter-visible text.
