# Pelican's Crook

A small fictional town on the Great Ocean Road, between Lorne and Apollo Bay. Named for the bend in the coastline (a "crook"), with a quiet triple meaning: crook (bend), crook (Australian slang: unwell), crook (criminal).

**Geography.** The town sits in a sheltered bay where a headland curls around to make the water calm enough for a jetty. Crook Creek runs down from the Otway hills behind town and meets the sea here. The town is hemmed in: ocean and cliffs on one side, steep bush-clad hills on the other. It can only spread along a thin coastal shelf — one main street parallel to the water, a few short streets climbing the slope behind it. A plotting asset: hard to slip into or out of unseen. The single road in is cut by floods and landslips each winter.

**History.** Founded as a salvage-and-rescue community — part lifesavers, part wreckers. The ambiguity is deliberate fuel: did the founding families heroically rescue survivors, or quietly profit from what washed ashore? Subsequent eras: timber (Otways), fishing, genteel guesthouse age, then the Great Ocean Road (built by returned soldiers post-WWI) made it a tourist route.

**Demographics.** A few hundred permanent residents, swelling to several thousand in summer. Permanent population skews older; thin working-family middle; restless cohort of young people who mostly leave. Fault lines: locals vs blow-ins; old timber-and-salvage families vs moneyed tree-changers; year-round workers vs absentee weekenders.

**Seasonal rhythm.** Meg arrives in autumn, as the town empties and the real community closes ranks — the pressure-cooker for a murder, because the suspect pool is small, local, and tangled.
