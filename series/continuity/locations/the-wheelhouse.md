# The Wheelhouse

Maggie's pottery studio and shopfront on Pelican's Crook main street. Home base for the series.

A shopfront means townsfolk wander in to browse, commission, complain, and gossip — funnelling the whole community past Maggie's bench. The wheel, the kiln, the glazes give tactile sensory writing and a rich vein of metaphor (firing, cracking, glaze that only reveals its true colour after the heat).

Glaze the cat decided The Wheelhouse is home in ch 1. The kiln is his personal heater.

**Recurring function.** The studio becomes an accidental incident room in ch 8: people come in to buy things they don't need and stay to talk. Maggie discovers she is the person the town is talking to — new enough to be neutral, with Neil's ear. The wheel and the clay are also the vehicle for Maggie's pottery-as-thinking practice: when an observation will not resolve, she returns to the bench and makes a provisional form. The form is never evidence or prophecy; it helps her see the story she is currently imposing and revise it when facts change.

**Book 01 studio body of work.** Do not make one bespoke suspect portrait per chapter. The recurring private/commission work should feel natural: Neil's ugly vase; a quiet divided Faye/Iris form; the distorted room-vessel after the cottage; cracked/repaired surf-club bowls; a light rope/coil experiment; a contained Mary-adjacent lidded vessel; and final bowls with repair-inspired glaze/tide lines. The final bowls should be handmade, varied within a coherent set, useful, properly priced, and clearly hers.

**Opening.** First described in ch 1: bare shelves, salt smell, light off the water — the specific relief of a woman who has stopped pretending. Ch 1–4 are setup/preparation; The Wheelhouse is not properly launched until the ch 5 open-studio afternoon, when locals enter the shop as guests/customers and the town begins treating it as a social room.

**Previous owner / future seed.** The frontage still carries former bait-and-tackle bones and an unresolved trace of the previous owner/tenant. Book 01 may mention this only as building history/local colour. Do not turn it into a Book 01 investigation; it is a future mystery seed.

## Established facts
- Ground floor of a narrow terrace; flat above, accessed separately (Maggie had seen it only in photographs by end of ch-01).
- Large front window with salt-hazed glass. Interior: concrete floor.
- Wheel in corner under a canvas cover. Kiln at the back — larger than Maggie expected; elements intact, no visible degradation, good contact; kiln wash left by previous occupant; cold and clean at ch-01 opening.
- Shelving: brackets and boards, pre-cut by Cal Burrell; fitted as three runs along the east wall in ch-01, spaced for tall pieces at bottom and smaller work above.
- Proper public launch: ch 5 open-studio afternoon, with Faye's food, Beryl's visitor-flow management, Dot/Glad arguing about the shop's previous life, Mary resetting cups/plates/tea towels, Cal at the edge, and Glaze disrupting the display.
