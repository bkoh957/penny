# The Wheelhouse

Maggie's pottery studio and shopfront on Pelican's Crook main street. Home base for the series.

A shopfront means townsfolk wander in to browse, commission, complain, and gossip — funnelling the whole community past Maggie's bench. The wheel, the kiln, the glazes give tactile sensory writing and a rich vein of metaphor (firing, cracking, glaze that only reveals its true colour after the heat).

Glaze the cat decided The Wheelhouse is home in ch 1. The kiln is his personal heater.

**Recurring function.** The studio becomes an accidental incident room in ch 8: people come in to buy things they don't need and stay to talk. Maggie discovers she is the person the town is talking to — new enough to be neutral, with Neil's ear. The wheel and the clay are also the vehicle for the gift's clay-as-externalised-sight technique: her hands sometimes rebuild a detail before her conscious mind catches up.

**Opening.** First described in ch 1: bare shelves, salt smell, light off the water — the specific relief of a woman who has stopped pretending.

## Established facts
- Ground floor of a narrow terrace; flat above, accessed separately (Maggie had seen it only in photographs by end of ch-01).
- Large front window with salt-hazed glass. Interior: concrete floor.
- Wheel in corner under a canvas cover. Kiln at the back — larger than Maggie expected; elements intact, no visible degradation, good contact; kiln wash left by previous occupant; cold and clean at ch-01 opening.
- Shelving: brackets and boards, pre-cut by Cal Burrell; fitted as three runs along the east wall in ch-01, spaced for tall pieces at bottom and smaller work above.
