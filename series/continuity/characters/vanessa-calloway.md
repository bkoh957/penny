# Vanessa Calloway

**Role:** Retired magistrate; dark horse suspect; keeper of buried history. First appears: ch 5.

Aboriginal Australian retired magistrate who still carries the authority of decades on the bench. Where Sergeant Pruitt handles the present, Vanessa Calloway embodies the town's legal past — old cases, old rulings, old grudges from people once sentenced or once let off. She knows the difference between truth, proof, justice and peace, and has no patience for outsiders who think one clever observation gives them all four.

She does not perform wisdom on demand. She is dry, watchful, formal when she wants distance, and slightly amused by people who think retired means harmless. She may buy one plain scone at the market and refuse jam because she has presided over enough disputes. She uses silence like a gavel and remembers dates better than names, especially names she disapproves of.

**Red herring role (chs 5–14).** Her gravitas makes her feel capable of anything. Her possible motive: Neil may have known of an old judgement Vanessa got wrong, or a favour that crossed a line. Her secret is real but unconnected to Neil's death.

**SERIES SEED — do not resolve in Book 01.** Calloway's buried bench skeleton is raised in ch 5 (skeleton at the old cemetery — hinted, not named) and left entirely unresolved. A thread for a future book. Leave it dangling.
