# Saffron

**Role:** Resented newcomer; major red herring; possible reluctant ally. First appears: ch 4.

Former corporate professional turned wellness-retreat owner. She left a city career about two years before Book 01 and arrived in Pelican's Crook selling executive burnout recovery, yoga, coastal reset packages and carefully packaged reinvention. That makes Maggie a mirror and a threat: another corporate woman arriving from the city to start over, only this time the town seems to be feeding her cake and sourdough instead of deciding against her before she had unpacked.

Saffron performs serenity because panic is bad for business. Out front: linen, green juice, crystals, breathwork, soft branding and controlled warmth. Out back: broken plumbing, instant coffee, unpaid invoices and retreat cashflow calculated to the dollar. She knows the wellness language is partly theatre; she also knows theatre pays the bills until it doesn't.

**Red herring role (chs 9–17).** She had loud motive (Neil was steering patients away from her retreat), no alibi she will share, and a history of small deceptions. Pruitt builds a case against her. The case collapses publicly ch 17: her alibi is humiliating but real — a meeting with a debt recovery agent in Colac at the time in question. She was not in Pelican's Crook. She was broke and ashamed of it. That is all.

**Post-vindication.** Ch 26: She accepts a cup of tea from Maggie in the studio without either of them making it significant. The beginning of something. A mirror for Maggie: another woman who came here to reinvent herself, resented Maggie at first because Maggie looked as if she might be allowed to do it more gently.

**Series seed.** A possible reluctant ally from Book 02 onward.

## Established facts
- Named to Maggie by Faye in ch 2: has "the wellness retreat up past the headland." Faye's characterisation: sells something Faye declined to name; "the town decided about her before she'd unpacked, and that's not always fair and isn't always wrong." Not seen in person in ch 2.
- Corporate background before Pelican's Crook: brand/communications/strategy/HR-adjacent professional works best. She understands how to package transformation because she used to package things for other people.
- Secret contradiction: drinks instant coffee in private while selling herbal cleanses; can say "abundance" with a straight face while knowing exactly which invoice will bounce first.
