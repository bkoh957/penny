# Mary Burrell

**Role:** Book 01 central figure. Cal's paternal aunt (née Burrell); daughter of the man whose death underlies the mystery. **Culprit identity is SEALED — do not write her role in the solution into any drafter-visible text.**

**Name:** Mary Burrell. Referred to in prose as "Cal's aunt" — her name is known to the reader but her role is not.

**For the drafter (visible facts only).**
- A woman in her sixties. Lifelong resident of Pelican's Crook.
- She has a decade-long, fixed belief that her father's death was not natural — dismissed by the town as her harmless fixation.
- She is warm, proprietary, familiar with the community hall kitchen; moves through it with the confidence of a woman who knows exactly where everything lives.
- She is in obvious distress at Neil Hartigan's death (ch 7), which the town reads as grief about her father's doctor, complicated by her old fixation.
- Cal worries about her quietly — she has been strange lately, stranger than her usual grief.
- She is raised as a possible person of interest early (ch 7, Pruitt glances at her) and dismissed as harmless. She hides behind this early exoneration.

**First on-page appearance:** ch 2 (brings Maggie a lemon-tree cutting from her late father's garden; sincerely kind, but studies her too carefully when Cal's name comes up). Returns ch 5 (community market — refolds a tea towel at the kitchen servery).

## Established facts
- First on-page appearance: ch 2. Arrived at Maggie's studio at four.
- Appearance: woman in her sixties; grey hair pinned back; cardigan the colour of wet sand; "neat in the way of women who have always been neat."
- Brought a terracotta pot holding a young lemon tree (two feet high, dark glossy leaves, a slip of something older grafted at the base) and a paper bag of six deep-yellow lemons from the same parent tree. Her late father planted the parent the year Mary was born; "it's still going." She takes cuttings for people she thinks might stay.
- Turned the pot a quarter so the best of it faced the light; tipped the lemons into the chipped blue bowl on Maggie's workbench.
- Said of Cal: "He measures everything twice. His father was the same." / "He's had a lot of people pass through. People come down here to fix their lives and then they go back up to the city to live them, and Cal's the one who builds the shelves they leave behind."
- Her eyes have the same "careful and steady" quality as Cal's; Maggie recognised the family resemblance.
- Final question on the way out, looking at the tree and not at Maggie: "How long are you thinking you'll stay? With the studio, I mean."
