# Artie Selwood

**Role:** Retired lighthouse keeper; living memory; the one who recognises the gift. First named: ch 16.

Full of embellished shipwreck history he will narrate to anyone he can corner — the 1878 wreck of the Loch Ard is a favourite — but underneath the tall tales he genuinely knows where every secret in this town is kept. He may be the one person who recognises what Maggie's episodes really are, because he saw the same thing once before, long ago.

**In Book 01:**
- Ch 16: Corners Maggie at the lighthouse. Very carefully tells her he once knew a woman who saw the way Maggie sees. Does not say more — runs out of steam or nerve, changes the subject to the Loch Ard. But the thing he said first stays with her through the migraine: *someone else, before her, saw this way. The gift has a history.*
- Ch 26: Sits in the corner of the pub and watches Maggie across the room with the expression of a man with more to say when the time is right.
- Ch 28: Comes in on the pretence of commissioning a mug; stays forty minutes circling something he is not quite saying about the woman he knew, and about the lighthouse, and about a record he has kept for thirty years. Leaves without saying it.

**Series role.** Slow-drip his recognition of Maggie's gift across several books. He is the key to the origin mystery of the Too-Much — do not resolve in Book 01.
