# Artie Selwood

**Role:** Recent arrival turned self-appointed local historian; lighthouse obsessive; the one who recognises the gift. First named: ch 16.

Artie seems, at first glance, as if he has been in Pelican's Crook forever. He has the lighthouse keys, the shipwreck anecdotes, the archive boxes, the habit of correcting tourist plaques and the confidence of a man who knows which storm did what to which jetty. In truth, he moved to town only five to ten years before Book 01. He belongs by study, collection and persistence, not by birth.

Full of embellished shipwreck history he will narrate to anyone he can corner — the 1878 wreck of the Loch Ard is a favourite — but underneath the tall tales he has genuinely learned where many secrets are kept. Locals tease him for acting ancient-local after five minutes; he accepts this as proof of progress. He is another reinvention model for Maggie: an outsider who tried to belong by learning the place obsessively until the place grudgingly made room.

**In Book 01:**
- Ch 16: Corners Maggie at the lighthouse. Very carefully tells her he once knew a woman who saw the way Maggie sees. Does not say more — runs out of steam or nerve, changes the subject to the Loch Ard. But the thing he said first stays with her through the migraine: *someone else, before her, saw this way. The gift has a history.*
- Ch 27: Sits in the corner of the pub and watches Maggie across the room with the expression of a man with more to say when the time is right.
- Ch 29: Comes in on the pretence of commissioning a mug; stays forty minutes circling something he is not quite saying about the woman he knew, and about the lighthouse, and about a record he has kept for years. Leaves without saying it.

**Series role.** Slow-drip his recognition of Maggie's gift across several books. He is the key to the origin mystery of the Too-Much — do not resolve in Book 01. His knowledge comes from obsessive local-history research, records and listening, not mystical ancient-local status.
