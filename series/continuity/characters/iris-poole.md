# Iris Poole

**Role:** The other party in the long-running jam feud with [[faye]]. Sells jam and preserves at the markets and judges the agricultural-show preserves competition Faye has entered for 22 years and never won. Named/introduced ch 2; first present on the page ch 5. Not a murder suspect.

Composed, exact, a touch formal — the kind of woman who labels everything and keeps her own counsel. At the autumn market her stall sits at the deliberate far end of the hall from Faye's display, a spatial fact every regular knows not to comment on.

**The feud's private wound.** The surface grudge is jam-show rivalry; underneath is an old, personal hurt that predates the competition. At the market (ch 5) Maggie's Migraine Sight reads this private motivation **accurately and aloud in public** — the chapter's overreach beat — and it costs her goodwill. The buried origin is a family crisis that [[neil-hartigan]] handled badly and later used as quiet leverage; this is seeded here and in ch 2 but **not explained until ch 13**, where it clears Faye and exposes Neil's pattern. Keep the origin off the page before ch 13: at ch 5 Maggie senses the wound, not its cause.

**Surname:** Poole. Known to the town as Iris.
