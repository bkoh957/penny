# Iris Poole

**Role:** The other party in the long-running jam feud with [[faye]]. Sells jam and preserves at the markets and has preserve prestige / judging power around the agricultural-show preserves competition Faye has entered for 22 years and never won. Named/introduced ch 2; first present on the page ch 5. Not a murder suspect.

Composed, exact, a touch formal — the kind of woman who labels everything and keeps her own counsel. At the autumn market her stall sits at the deliberate far end of the hall from Faye's display, a spatial fact every regular knows not to comment on.

**The feud's private tension.** The surface grudge is jam-show rivalry; underneath is an old, personal hurt that predates the competition. Faye's mother made one last preserve entry during a family crisis/illness, and Iris, as steward/judge, disqualified it on a rule-bound technicality. Iris is not villainous; she may have been following rules and may regret the cost. At the open-studio launch (ch 5) the Too-Much reads the social geography of the tension, but Maggie catches herself and does **not** publicly expose it; Chapter 5 is an almost-mistake, not a breach. Keep the origin off the page before ch 12: at ch 5 Maggie sees jar placement, avoided paths, halted jokes and rule-bound politeness, not the cause.

**Surname:** Poole. Known to the town as Iris.
