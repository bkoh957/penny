# Iris Poole

**Role:** The other party in the long-running jam feud with [[faye]]. Sells jam and preserves at the markets and has preserve prestige / judging power around the agricultural-show preserves competition Faye has entered for years. Named/introduced ch 2; first present on the page ch 5. Not a murder suspect.

Composed, exact, a touch formal — the kind of woman who labels everything and keeps her own counsel. At the autumn market her stall sits at the deliberate far end of the hall from Faye's display, a spatial fact every regular knows not to comment on. She labels jars with museum-quality handwriting, gives exact change before being asked, and corrects preserve terminology under her breath.

**The jam feud.** Iris sells/serves the same jam Faye serves at the bakery. Each believes her version is the proper version; the town treats the duplicate jam as comedy because choosing sides is safer than discussing the old wound beneath it. Underneath is an old, personal hurt: Faye's mother's recipe / preserve entry, a failed seal or judging note, and Iris's rule-bound role in a disqualification during a family crisis. Iris is not villainous; she may have been following rules, may have tried to reduce worse embarrassment, and may regret the cost. She may have kept the old recipe card, label, ribbon or judging note because she could not bear to throw it away.

At the open-studio launch (ch 5) the Too-Much reads the social geography of the tension, but Maggie catches herself and does **not** publicly expose it; Chapter 5 is an almost-mistake, not a breach. Keep the origin off the page before ch 12: at ch 5 Maggie sees jar placement, avoided paths, halted jokes, duplicate-jam awkwardness and rule-bound politeness, not the cause.

**Surname:** Poole. Known to the town as Iris.
