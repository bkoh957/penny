# Faye Denton

**Role:** Maggie's best friend and exposition engine. Runs the bakery. First appears: ch 2, but she and Maggie already know each other lightly from Maggie's earlier Great Ocean Road pottery-trail visit.

Asian-Australian bakery owner: friendly, generous and socially perceptive, but easily anxious under the warmth. Feeding people is both kindness and self-management — it lets her care, delay, observe, and avoid saying the naked thing too quickly. She knows everyone's business going back three generations and can deliver town context with bread in her hands, but she worries about overstepping, misreading, or becoming the person who turns private hurt into public entertainment.

Faye is not fragile. She is competent, beloved and funny; the anxiety lives under the competence. She over-explains when nervous, labels pantry shelves too carefully when upset, and uses bakery work to avoid eye contact. Her staff member Tania is more relaxed and unflappable, which makes their bakery dynamic warmer and more comic: Faye worries aloud; Tania has already handled the ovens, the queue and the customer who wanted yesterday's loaf.

**The jam feud.** Faye serves a specific house jam at the bakery; Iris Poole sells/serves the same jam at markets. Each insists her version is the proper one, and the town has turned the dispute into a standing joke: customers choose sides, Dot and Glad disagree with religious seriousness, and Beryl periodically tries to ban jam arguments from committee mornings. The feud hides a genuine old wound around Faye's mother's recipe, a preserve entry, a failed seal / judging note, and whose version of the story the town chose to remember. This surfaces in ch 12 and is cleared warmly. Her secret is sad and old and not a murder.

**Red herring role.** Ch 12: Maggie follows the thread because she wants to clear Faye, but friendship gives context, not ownership. The uncomfortable conversation is intimate rather than public; they drink tea in silence afterward and neither mentions it again. The friendship survives.

**Surname:** Denton. Known to the whole town as Faye; "Mrs Denton" is what Beryl calls her when she wants to be pointed about something.

## Established facts
- Maggie met Faye four years before Book 01 on a Great Ocean Road pottery-trail visit. They clicked warmly enough to keep a light thread of contact; this old welcome was one reason Maggie chose Pelican's Crook after the divorce.
- First on-page appearance: ch 2. Arrived at Maggie's studio at nine with a sourdough loaf ("a good loaf but it's not a patient one"); broke it with her hands rather than waiting for a knife.
- Tania minds the ovens and does not panic, even when Faye says she does. Ovens go on at six; bread gone by half past eight; serious regulars arrive by eight.
- Introduced Maggie to the town's social map in order: Dot and Glad, Cobber, Beryl Foss, Saffron, Mara, Mary Kearney, and Iris Poole.
- Her manner when Iris Poole's name arose: went briefly, precisely closed — "the warmth still sitting on the surface like oil on water and nothing underneath it." Said only: "Iris does very good jam. Everyone says so." Sealed the subject.
- Book 01 red-herring payoff: the jam material should leave positive clue gain, not only family dignity. A recipe card, show schedule, committee minute, entry label or clipping can provide a date that points toward the year Mary’s father died and Neil’s abrupt change afterwards.
- Offered to send customers to The Wheelhouse ("the ones who'd actually buy a nice bowl instead of telling you it's dear") once Maggie learns the town first. Advice: "Don't come at it with a story about yourself ready-made."
- Left flour on Maggie's clean floor without noticing; departed promptly.
