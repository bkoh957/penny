# Dot

**Role:** One half of the op-shop surveillance duo. First appears: ch 2.

Runs the op-shop / general store with Glad. Elderly; the town's unofficial CCTV. Dot and Glad miss nothing that happens on the main street. The catch: they are not related, despite looking uncannily alike after decades of shared habits, matching cardigans, identical glasses and mutual stubbornness. They deny the resemblance with great offence, often while making the same gesture at the same time.

Dot and Glad have not agreed on a single fact since 1987. Their testimony agrees on the large facts and diverges on every detail (the car was dark blue — Dot; dark green — Glad). Dot's lane: every object has a story, and she remembers emotional/social context better than practical details. Glad's lane: every object has a price, and she remembers transactions better than sentiment.

**In Book 01:** Their contradictory account of a car parked briefly outside Neil's is a necessary clue (ch 11). The real fact buried beneath their disagreement: a car, unusual for that street at that hour. The colour dispute is Dot/Glad at their finest — the truth requires adjudication.

**Note.** Dot and Glad are best written as a unit with individual lanes: not sisters, not cousins, not related; just a long-running civic double-act who have become visually and socially twinned by time.

## Established facts
- Named to Maggie by Faye in ch 2: run the op-shop on the main street together. Faye's characterisation: "between them see everything that moves on the street and agree on none of it." Advice: "If you ever need to know what happened, ask them both and split the difference. If you want a quiet life, don't ask them anything." Not seen in person in ch 2.
