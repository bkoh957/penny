# Vincent Calloway

**Role:** Retired magistrate; dark horse suspect; keeper of buried history. First appears: ch 5.

A retired magistrate who still carries the authority of decades on the bench. Where Sergeant Pruitt handles the present, Calloway embodies the town's legal past — old cases, old rulings, old grudges from people once sentenced or once let off. A walking archive of motive. Knows where bodies are buried, figuratively and — given the old cemetery — perhaps not only figuratively.

**Red herring role (chs 5–14).** His gravitas makes him feel capable of anything. His motive: Neil may have known of an old judgement Calloway got wrong, or a favour that crossed a line. His secret is real but unconnected to Neil's death.

**SERIES SEED — do not resolve in Book 01.** Calloway's buried bench skeleton is raised in ch 5 (skeleton at the old cemetery — hinted, not named) and left entirely unresolved. A thread for a future book. Leave it dangling.
