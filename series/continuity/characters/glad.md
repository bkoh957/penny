# Glad

**Role:** One half of the op-shop surveillance duo. First appears: ch 2.

Runs the op-shop / general store with Dot. She and Dot look alike enough that newcomers assume they are sisters; they are not related and both consider the assumption lazy observation. Their resemblance comes from decades of shared routines, matching cardigans, identical glasses and the town's refusal to imagine one without the other.

Glad's testimony on any given afternoon never quite matches Dot's: the car was dark green (Dot said dark blue), it arrived at twenty to eight (Dot said half past seven). The single real buried fact that emerges from their combined testimony: a car, unusual for that street at that hour, parked briefly outside Neil's. Neither woman knows whose it was.

**Lane:** Dot believes every object has a story; Glad believes every object has a price and most stories are lies. Glad is the more ruthless pricer, better at transaction details, worse at admitting sentiment.

**Note.** Dot and Glad are best written as a unit with distinct lanes: not sisters, not cousins, not related; they are a surveillance installation the town accidentally built out of two unrelated women who disagree for sport.
