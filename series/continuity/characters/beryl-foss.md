# Beryl Foss

**Role:** Social gatekeeper; CWA chair; comic antagonist (affectionate). First appears: ch 2.

Runs the town's social calendar with an iron fist, a clipboard, and a smaller clipboard that is somehow worse. Every fundraiser, market, and funeral runs through her. She is the institutional power of the town — to be accepted in Pelican's Crook is to be accepted by Beryl. Did Not Approve of the newcomer.

Beryl can silence a hall by adjusting the urn plug. She knows who under-donated to the raffle in 2009, which trestle table pinches fingers, and exactly how many slices a cake should yield if cut by someone with community spirit. She says "I'm not interfering" immediately before interfering. She is not anti-newcomer; she is anti-flimsy. If Maggie proves useful and reliable, Beryl becomes terrifyingly loyal.

**Red herring role (chs 10–14).** She was overheard arguing with Neil in the car park behind the bakery; her manner under questioning goes very still. Her secret: Neil gave her a diagnosis she has told nobody, and the argument was her demanding he keep it out of her file and his refusing, gently, because he could not in good conscience. The iron matriarch is mortal and does not want the town to know. Cleared ch 14/17 depending on final outline rhythm.

**Arc.** Thaws from obstacle to fierce, prickly ally across the series. Her illness humanises her without diminishing her authority.

**Note.** Her events (markets, fundraisers, hall clean-ups) provide the warm community set-pieces every book needs — and a stage for Maggie's gift to misfire publicly.

## Established facts
- Named to Maggie by Faye in ch 2: runs the Country Women's Association (CWA). Faye's characterisation: "She'll find you. Don't go looking — let her do it her way. And be straight with her. She can smell a person managing her from across a hall." Not seen in person in ch 2.
