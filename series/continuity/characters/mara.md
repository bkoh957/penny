# Mara

**Role:** Coastal land-care / dune-restoration figure; headland/developer thread; community texture. First appears: ch 2.

White Australian local/semi-local who cares deeply about the coast in the practical, unromantic way of someone who has spent years repairing damage other people call scenery. She works with dune stakes, seed trays, erosion fencing, council forms, volunteer rosters, dogs-off-leash arguments, tourist shortcuts and developer language that treats living coast as vacant opportunity.

Mara is not there to give lyrical speeches about the land. She is blunt, sunburned, usually carrying cable ties or gloves, and has limited patience for people who love the view but ignore the fence. She respects Traditional Owners and local Aboriginal groups and is careful not to speak for them; her authority is practical restoration work, not cultural ownership. She makes Pelican's Crook feel like real coastal Australia rather than a postcard.

**Function.** Grounds the town beyond shops, gossip and suspect business. Her headland/developer conflict remains community texture in Book 01 unless promoted later. She gives Maggie a non-murder lesson: loving a place means boring maintenance, not just admiring it.

## Established facts
- First seen by Maggie in ch 2 near the foreshore/dunes with restoration gear: gloves, stakes, bags, clipboard, seedlings or orange mesh.
- In a calm but firm disagreement with someone mentioning developer interest in the headland or a plan that treats the foreshore as vacant opportunity.
- Faye frames her as someone whose work matters and whose patience has limits.
