# Thread: C — Internal (Maggie's arc)

A woman who stopped seeing her own life honestly learns to see again. Maggie fled a stale marriage and a career spent absorbing other people's problems. Her arc in Book 01: from hiding in a coastal town and apologising for taking up space, to knowing she belongs here. The pottery is not a quirky occupation — it is a passion she set aside for a sensible life and is only now returning to. The Too-Much and this arc are the same journey in two costumes: recovering from a life spent not looking honestly, and learning that a crack can reveal process without becoming a verdict.

- Status: OPEN (Book 01, ch 01 — multi-book arc, not resolved here).
- Key beats: arrival and specific relief ch 1; binge-and-hangover chs 5–6; the solo investigation as a form of seeing truly chs 7–23; the kiln batch ch 29.
- The binge (ch 5) is a character flaw in action: using the gift to be liked mirrors staying in the stale marriage to feel safe.
- **The Tommy-voice is the concrete mechanism of this arc.** "Apologising for taking up space" is literal: short flashback bursts of her ex-husband's belittling lines fire at confidence thresholds, especially around art, cracks, backstory and the fear that she turns ordinary objects into drama. Sting ch1–18; they **pivot to fuel at the ch19 click** (the sight that exposed Tommy becomes her strength); spur ch19–29. Coda ch29: the voice has gone quiet and she stamps her pots "Quill" — reclaiming the name. Cross-ref: [[thomas-quill]].
