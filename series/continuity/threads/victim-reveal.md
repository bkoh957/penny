# Thread: Victim-Reveal (Neil's three-movement arc)

Dr Neil Hartigan is not a static victim. His character is revealed in three movements across Book 01:

1. **Saint (chs 1–8).** Introduced warm and alive; the town's beloved anchor. Mourned universally when he dies.
2. **Sinner (chs 9–17).** Evidence emerges that Neil had been reviewing old records — a death from a decade ago. Whispers that he may not have been what the town thought. Even Maggie wobbles.
3. **Person (chs 18–26).** The fuller truth: it was a begged-for mercy, the most loving thing anyone did for the dying man. The town's grief re-complicates. A saint is too simple; the truth is harder and warmer than that.

- Status: OPEN (ch 1) → RESOLVES (ch 26).
- The arc resolves at the informal pub gathering (ch 26), where people say things about Neil that are more complicated than the first time.
