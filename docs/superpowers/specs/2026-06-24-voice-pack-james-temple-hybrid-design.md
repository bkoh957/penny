# Voice Pack Design — Clive James / Peter Temple Hybrid
Date: 2026-06-24

## Purpose

Replace the thin three-bullet voice pack with a substantive style brief that guides the drafter, line-editor, and inspector-voice toward a consistent, genre-appropriate register. The voice is a **70% Clive James (travel-writer mode) / 30% Peter Temple** hybrid that modulates under narrative pressure.

## Genre contract (load-bearing)

This is a cozy mystery. The voice must make the reader want to stay in the world. Death is present but the puzzle — the social pleasure of working it out — is the frame. Warmth is not decoration; it is the engine. Nothing in this voice pack overrides that contract. Temple's darkness and menace are absent entirely; his economy and Australian-ness come in.

---

## Six sections

### 1. Observer stance

The protagonist moves through her world the way a travel writer moves through a foreign country: noticing what locals stopped seeing, finding the particular detail that stands for the general condition. This is James's mode — the outsider eye, not the alienated eye. Curiosity, not detachment. The reader feels invited into the observation, not excluded from it.

The stance generates warmth organically: when the narrator finds something remarkable in an ordinary thing, the reader shares the discovery.

### 2. Sentence construction

Default register is James: compound sentences that build, with subordinate clauses that earn their length. The surprise is in the arrival — the sentence sets up a direction and lands somewhere slightly unexpected.

Temple enters as **punctuation**: a short declarative after a longer construction, not the other way around. One blunt sentence can end a paragraph. Never three short sentences in a row unless tension is rising (see §5).

Rule of thumb: if a paragraph has four sentences of roughly equal length, at least one is wrong.

### 3. Detail philosophy

Specific over general, always. James's travel writing earns its generalisations by arriving at them through a particular: not "the town was run-down" but the specific detail that carries run-down as a conclusion the reader draws themselves.

Simile and metaphor must illuminate, not decorate. If the comparison doesn't change how the reader sees the thing, cut it.

Avoid abstracted emotion. The protagonist doesn't feel warmth; she notices the thing that causes it.

### 4. Australian register (Temple)

The prose sounds Australian without announcing it. Idiom at the word and phrase level — vernacular ease, local colour. The protagonist thinks in Australian; she does not translate for an overseas reader. Place names, flora, fauna, and social customs are named without apology or footnote.

This is Temple's primary contribution to the base register: not his plot mechanics, not his darkness, but the cadence and diction that grounds the novel in its place.

### 5. Register under pressure

As the novel moves toward its climax, Temple takes over progressively. The shift is gradual, not switched:

- Rising tension: subordinate clauses reduce, sentences shorten, the narration becomes less ruminative
- Peak tension: sentences stop building. Things happen and the prose reports them. No wit until the pressure drops
- The climax is a revelation, not a confrontation — the tightening is about cognitive and emotional urgency, not menace or violence

The cozy contract holds through the climax: even at maximum Temple, the register stays on the right side of dark.

After the revelation, the voice returns toward James — the protagonist (and the reader) can breathe again, and the observation resumes.

### 6. What not to do

- **No BBC dryness**: James's wit is warm; it does not perform cleverness at the reader's expense
- **No American crime cadence**: hard-boiled is a different genre
- **No over-explanation**: neither James nor Temple explains what the detail already shows
- **No stated sentiment**: earned through observation, not announced ("she felt sad" is not a sentence in this book)
- **No noir menace**: Temple's darkness stays out
- **No gush**: warmth ≠ effusiveness
