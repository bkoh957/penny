# Book 01 — Suspect-Arc Restructure: the Cal False Lead (design)

## Problem

Book 01's back half clears its suspects **serially** — Faye (ch 12), Calloway (ch 14),
Saffron (ch 17), with Beryl set aside at ch 10 — until ch 18 declares "the last suspect is
spent," then Mary clicks at ch 19–20. Two craft costs follow:

1. **A suspense vacuum at ch 17→19:** once Saffron collapses there is no live suspect and
   Mary has not yet clicked; the stretch rides entirely on the gift/romance arc.
2. **Guilt-by-remainder risk:** visible one-by-one elimination leaks suspense, and because
   Mary is never a *tracked* suspect (only a clue-bearer), her reveal leans hard on the
   planted habit-clue to feel fair rather than bolt-from-blue.

The book's engine is *hidden-in-plain-sight* (Mary is the warm helper-aunt; her guilt-clue —
the precision **resetting habit** — is planted as texture at ch 5 and ch 7), so a "clear
everyone only at the climax" rewrite would fight the design. The fix keeps the hidden
culprit and instead routes the late-game dread through the love interest.

## Goal

Replace the serial fade-out with a **three-stage collapse-and-converge** that ends the
suspense vacuum and makes the Mary reveal land through, not despite, the reader's fear for
Cal. Culprit, victim, motive, weapon, and reveal chapter are unchanged; this re-times
*resolutions* and elevates an already-seeded red herring (Cal). Chapter count stays 29; the
finalized ch 1–2 are untouched.

## Decisions (locked with the showrunner)

1. **Cal-as-suspect shape: evidence converges, Maggie fights it with discipline.** The
   physical evidence (the staged scene's precision habit + Cal's motive: Neil planting doubt
   about the workshop sale) genuinely points at Cal. Maggie is terrified it is him and spends
   the discipline she *failed* with Beryl and Saffron to **not accuse** — she seeks proof.
   The proof that clears Cal is the proof that reveals Mary. Pruitt, independently, had the
   same thought and cleared Cal on proof — confirming her restraint was sound, not love-blind.
2. **The dead-end pair is Saffron + Beryl.** Both carry real motive and a private,
   guilt-shaped secret that resolves to non-criminal pain at nearly the same time, producing
   a true wall. Calloway (series seed) and Faye (wound→repair arc) are untouched.
3. **Mary stays passive — she never points at Cal.** The cruel irony is *structural*: Cal
   looks guilty because Mary **raised him and taught him every habit** ("she made him who he
   is"), not because she frames him. Mary remains a tragic mercy-killer, not a manipulator.
4. **Cal learns he was suspected (ch 24).** In the rupture, Maggie admits she feared it was
   him and chose proof over accusation — reframing the suspicion as the thing that *protected*
   him. The rupture cuts deeper; the ch 28 repair is earned and specific.

## The three-stage spine

- **Stage 1 — Dead-end (two live possibles, both fizzle).** Saffron and Beryl run as
  parallel suspects; both resolve to private, non-criminal secrets near-simultaneously.
  Maggie hits a wall: not "everyone's cleared" but "my two best answers both evaporated."
- **Stage 2 — Cal emerges.** With the obvious suspects gone, the precision habit + Cal's
  motive converge on the man Maggie loves. Dark moment routed through her arc: discipline,
  not accusation; seek proof. Pruitt's independent same-thought runs in parallel.
- **Stage 3 — Cal clears = Mary converges.** Cobber places the blue-green car (planted ch 9)
  in the timeline gap — **Mary's, not Cal's**; Cal's own gap is independently accounted — and
  the habit's origin surfaces: Mary raised Cal. The habit that read as Cal's was hers all
  along. Cal clears on proof; Mary lands.

The misdirection axis flips from "which cleared suspect did it" to "the culprit hides inside
the reader's fear for Cal." No vacuum; Mary stays fair-but-hidden (her ch 5/7 clue pays off).
