# Design — Tommy Quill, the ghost in Maggie's head

**Date:** 2026-06-28
**Status:** Approved (brainstorming) → ready for implementation plan
**Scope:** Series-canon character + Book 01 internal-arc device. Touches continuity
bibles/threads, the Book 01 outline, the style-sheet, and a prose retrofit of
ch-01/02 (finalized) and ch-03–05 (drafts).

## Problem

The novel is about a 40+ divorcee remaking her life, but the ex-husband is currently a
ghost only in the weak sense: he is never named and barely present (`maggie-quill.md`,
`series-bible.md`, and `ch-01.final.md` say only "the marriage," "the divorce," "raw
from the divorce"). We want his presence felt throughout — *a ghost in her mind* — while
staying inside cozy-mystery tropes. He must shape Maggie without ever appearing on-page
in Book 01.

## The character (series-canon, off-page in Book 01)

**Thomas "Tommy" Quill** — real-estate broker, Maggie's ex-husband.

- **Temperament:** jealous of her competence; a *belittler of the plausible kind*. Never
  slurs, never violence — the "I'm just being honest, love" condescension that corrodes
  precisely because it sounds reasonable and helpful. This is the cozy-safe register and
  it is load-bearing: the menace is emotional and diminishing, never abusive.
- **The end of the marriage:** the affair was real. Maggie's *waking* gift (see origin
  reconciliation below) decoded it — the first thing it ever made her understand. She was
  willing to forgive; **he** turned it into anger and divorced **her**.
- **On-page rule (Book 01):** Tommy never appears — no phone call, no letter, no scene.
  He exists only as quoted memory inside Maggie's head. He is seeded as a *possible*
  future on-page presence for later books (a series-seed), but Book 01 never lets him in
  the room.
- **The kept name (load-bearing detail):** Maggie is still **Maggie *Quill***. She kept
  his surname. The ghost is literally in her signature, and the Book 01 finale pays this
  off (see Arc → Coda).

## Canon reconciliation — the Too-Much origin

Existing canon (`canon-core.md`, `gift.md`, `series-bible.md`, `maggie-quill.md`) says
Maggie has had the migraines / the Too-Much **since she was nineteen — "her whole life."**
The brief said it "began after 40." Resolution (chosen): **migraines old, reading new.**

- Migraines since 19 — **unchanged.**
- After 40 the Too-Much **sharpened from *recording* to *reading*** — meaning began to
  arrive, not just detail.
- **Tommy's affair was the first thing the gift ever decoded.** The gift and the divorce
  become one origin wound: she left the marriage because she finally *saw*.
- This does **not** open the gift's full origin — that remains Artie Selwood's
  series-long mystery (`series-seeds.md`). This is only "the first thing it ever made
  mean something." Keep the touch light; do not over-explain the gift here.

## The device on the page

**Short flashback bursts** — concrete, quoted *Tommy*, dropped into Maggie's interiority.

- **Form:** 1–2 sentences of his actual speech, no scene-break, surfacing at a trigger.
- **Treatment convention:** italics (set as a rule in the style-sheet so it stays
  consistent across drafters and books).
- **Register (calibration samples):**
  - *"You're not really a 'pottery' person, Mags. You're a person who likes the idea of it."*
  - *"Leave the clever stuff to people who do it for a living."*
- **Guardrails (cozy):** emotional/diminishing only; always short; never gratuitous;
  never derails a scene's warmth for long; always nets toward her growth.
- **The relief tie-in:** the wheel is the one place his voice cannot reach. This plugs
  directly into existing relief canon (`gift.md` / `the-wheelhouse.md`: centring clay is
  her only reliable relief from the Too-Much).

## The arc: sting → (ch19 pivot) → spur

The "disturb her confidence, then spur her on" turn is the spine. The turn **pivots on
the gift's 'click' at ch19** — the same beat that solves the murder.

- **Phase 1 — sting (ch1–18).** Bursts fire at *confidence thresholds*: taking up space,
  being liked (loudest at the **ch5 binge**, her flaw in action), risking a deduction,
  and especially when **Cal** treats her kindly (she does not trust being treated well).
  The lines stop her, shrink her, make her apologise for occupying space.
- **Pivot — ch19, the click.** The gift cracks the case. Maggie realizes *the sight that
  exposed Tommy is now her strength.* A Tommy-line surfaces and, for the first time,
  **powers** her instead of stopping her. Voice-arc and case-arc resolve on the same beat.
- **Phase 2 — spur (ch19–29).** The same belittling lines now land as fuel — she does the
  thing precisely *because* he'd mock it.
- **Coda — ch29, the kiln finale.** She notices the voice has gone quiet, and **stamps
  her finished pots "Quill"** — reclaiming the name as *hers*, not his. (The kept-name
  detail from above is the payoff.)

The *relationship's* full closure (does Tommy ever reappear?) is deliberately **not**
resolved in Book 01 — it is a series-seed.

## Anchor placements

Bursts are planted (not every chapter — at thresholds):

- **ch1** — arrival / signing the lease "in a state she would later describe as quietly
  deranged" (existing line is a natural hook).
- **ch2** — settling-in / first real warmth from the town.
- **ch5–6** — the binge and the hangover (flaw in action; the deepest sting).
- **ch7** — the keystone flood (gift recording without understanding).
- **mid-book** — an assertion beat where she risks a deduction.
- **ch19** — the pivot (see Arc).
- **ch24** — Cal's wound; the voice tempts her to retreat into smallness.
- **ch29** — the coda (voice quiet + name reclaim).

## Out of scope / non-goals

- Tommy on-page in Book 01 (off-page rule).
- Resolving the gift's full origin (Artie's series mystery).
- Resolving whether Tommy ever returns (series-seed).
- Any abusive/violent register — emotional diminishment only.

## Success criteria

- A reader feels Tommy's presence throughout Book 01 without him ever appearing.
- The sting→spur turn is legible and pivots on ch19.
- The ch29 name-reclaim lands as a payoff of a planted detail.
- All continuity stays consistent (migraines-since-19 preserved; flaw-rhyme preserved).
- Retrofitted chapters pass the review gate and re-finalize cleanly.
