# Mystery Solution — Book 01: The Pelican's Crook Mysteries
<!-- SEALED — drafter-invisible. Only the book-scaffolder and the showrunner may read this. -->

## The Culprit

**Mary Burrell** — Tom's paternal aunt; née Burrell; daughter of the dying man. A woman in her sixties, lifelong resident of Pelican's Crook. She has spent a decade believing her father was murdered — a suspicion the town dismissed as her harmless fixation. A discovery (Neil's private notes describing the old man's repeated, desperate pleas, and the eased passing) finally named Neil Hartigan as the hand behind her father's death. She confronted him and killed him believing she was avenging a crime. She was right that his death was no accident. She was catastrophically wrong about what it meant.

## The Victim

**Dr Neil Hartigan**, town GP. Beloved, long-serving. He performed a mercy death on the aunt's father a decade ago — at the old man's own repeated, explicit plea — and signed the death certificate as natural. He carried the secret for years. In his late-life honesty he had been reviewing old records in the months before his death, and his private notes found their way to the aunt. His compassion is the thing that killed him.

## The Central Deception

Mary enters Neil's cottage as a familiar visitor — she has been there many times over the years; she knows where everything lives. After she kills him she erases her presence: washes the second cup, refolds the tea towel, squares the chair. A normal observer sees a tidy room. Meg's Migraine Sight sees the seams of the cleaning.

## The Keystone Clue — The Erasure

**Planted (ch 7):** Meg is struck by a migraine outside Neil's cottage. In the flood she perceives not what is there but what is wrong about a room that has been smoothed: a chair squared a fraction too neatly while Neil's own sits askew; a faint ring on the wood where a second cup stood; the cup washed and returned a fraction out of Neil's habitual order; a tea towel damp and refolded; the biscuit tin lid fractionally misaligned. She records: *the room has been erased.* She does not yet understand.

**The keystone within the keystone:** The washed cup was dried and returned with the unthinking, proprietary familiarity of someone who knows this kitchen as well as their own. Not a stranger — someone intimate with Neil and the house, from history and family.

**Click (ch 19):** At a CWA hall clean-up, Meg watches the aunt dry a cup and put it away with that exact gesture — the unthinking, proprietary certainty of someone who knows where everything belongs. The photograph develops. She understands what she saw through Neil's window: not two cups, but the signature of a person who belonged there, erasing herself.

## The Revelation Arc

- **ch 19:** The cup click. Meg understands who erased the room.
- **ch 20:** Meg assembles the timeline — Cobber's dawn sighting, Dot/Glad's car, the aunt's familiarity with Neil's cottage.
- **ch 21:** Meg finds Neil's private notes. The mercy is now legible; the aunt's fatal misreading is legible.
- **ch 22:** Meg takes it to Pruitt, who listens with the expression of a man who wants to say no and cannot quite.
- **ch 23:** Meg tells Tom before Pruitt moves. The worst conversation in the book.
- **ch 24:** Meg and Pruitt go to the aunt's house. She opens the door before they knock — she has been waiting. The arrest is quiet.

## The Series Wound

The aunt is Tom's paternal aunt; her father (the dying man) was Tom's grandfather. The romance survives but is wounded — she followed her gift into his family and it cost him something permanent. This is the slow-burn fuel for books 2+.
